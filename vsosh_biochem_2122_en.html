Task 4

Specific activity of lipase in the enzyme preparation

5 pts ▼
Experimental setup. 10 g of neutral fat (triacylglyceride) was dissolved in 1000 mL of 0.1% aqueous sodium deoxycholate. To 500 mL of this emulsion, 250 µL of the lipase preparation was added, and the mixture was incubated at 25 °C. Aliquots were taken at 5 min (Sample 1) and 10 min (Sample 2), and the reaction was stopped by heating.

Using the protein concentration from Task 2 and the acid concentrations from Task 3, calculate lipase activity in µmol neutral fat hydrolysed per minute per mg of protein.
Activity calculation — step by step
Algorithm:
1. Δc(acid) = c(S2) − c(S1) [mM] — change in acid concentration between the two aliquots.
2. In 1 mL aliquot: Δn(acid) = Δc × 0.001 L × 1000 = Δc µmol.
3. Aliquots represent 1 mL out of 500 mL total reaction volume → n(acid, total) = Δn × 500 µmol.
4. Δt = 10 − 5 = 5 min → rate = n(acid, total) / 5 [µmol/min].
5. Protein mass in reaction = c(protein) [mg/mL] × 0.250 mL = ? mg.
6. Specific activity = rate / m(protein) [µmol/min/mg].
Δc(acid) = c(S2) − c(S1): mM
Δn(acid) in total reaction (500 mL): µmol
Reaction rate (÷ 5 min): µmol/min
Protein mass (250 µL × 7.1 mg/mL): mg
Specific activity: µmol neutral fat / min / mg protein
Using example values from the virtual lab (your values may differ slightly):
c(S1) ≈ 21.0 mM, c(S2) ≈ 37.5 mM → Δc = 16.5 mM
Δn in 1 mL = 16.5 µmol; in 500 mL total = 16.5 × 500 = 8250 µmol
Rate = 8250 / 5 = 1650 µmol/min
Protein mass = 7.1 mg/mL × 0.250 mL = 1.775 mg
Specific activity = 1650 / 1.775 ≈ 930 µmol/min/mg
(Exact value depends on your titration readings.)
Task 5

Molecular weight of the neutral fat

5 pts ▼
A 1.21 g sample of the neutral fat used in this work was subjected to complete alkaline hydrolysis by boiling it with 100 mL of 100 mM NaOH (reflux condenser). After hydrolysis was complete, the concentration of unreacted NaOH was determined by titration and found to be 55 mM.

Calculate the molecular weight of the neutral fat.
Molecular weight calculation
Key concept: A neutral fat (triacylglycerol, TAG) requires 3 mol of NaOH for complete saponification:
TAG + 3 NaOH → glycerol + 3 R-COONa
n(NaOH) initial = 0.100 L × 100 mM: mmol
n(NaOH) remaining = 0.100 L × 55 mM: mmol
n(NaOH) consumed = initial − remaining: mmol
n(TAG) = n(NaOH consumed) ÷ 3: mmol
M(TAG) = 1.21 g ÷ n(TAG): g/mol
n(NaOH)initial = 0.1 L × 0.1 mol/L = 10 mmol
n(NaOH)remaining = 0.1 L × 0.055 mol/L = 5.5 mmol
n(NaOH)consumed = 10 − 5.5 = 4.5 mmol
n(TAG) = 4.5 / 3 = 1.5 mmol = 0.0015 mol
M = 1.21 g / 0.0015 mol = 807 g/mol

For reference: tripalmitin C₅₁H₉₈O₆ has M = 807 g/mol ✓
(Triolein C₅₇H₁₀₄O₆ would give M = 885 g/mol)
✓ Correct! M(neutral fat) = 807 g/mol — consistent with tripalmitin.
