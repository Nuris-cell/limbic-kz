Task 4

Specific activity of lipase in the enzyme preparation

5 points ▼
Calculate lipase activity in µmol neutral fat / min / mg protein, using data from Tasks 2 and 3.

Key data: 250 µL enzyme preparation was added to 500 mL emulsion. Aliquots = 1 mL each.
Activity calculation
Algorithm:
1. n(acid in 1 mL aliquot) = c(acid) × 0.001 L → µmol
2. Total reaction volume = 500 mL → n(total) = n(1 mL) × 500
3. Rate = Δn / Δt (µmol/min)
4. Protein mass in reaction = c(protein) × 0.25 mL → mg
5. Specific activity = Rate / m(protein) [µmol/min/mg]

Example with c(acid)₁ = 21.0 mM, c₂ = 37.5 mM:
Δc = 16.5 mM in 1 mL → Δn = 16.5 µmol
In 500 mL: 16.5 × 500 = 8250 µmol
Rate = 8250 / 5 = 1650 µmol/min
Protein mass: 7.1 mg/mL × 0.25 mL = 1.775 mg
Specific activity ≈ 930 µmol/min/mg (varies with titration results)
Task 5

Molecular weight of the neutral fat

5 points ▼
A 1.21 g sample of neutral fat was completely hydrolysed by boiling in 100 mL of 100 mM NaOH. After hydrolysis, the concentration of unreacted NaOH was found by titration to be 55 mM. Calculate the molecular weight of the fat.
Molecular weight calculation
Key concept: A neutral fat (triacylglycerol, TAG) reacts with 3 mol NaOH:
TAG + 3 NaOH → glycerol + 3 fatty acid Na⁺

n(NaOH initial) = 100 mM × 0.1 L = 10 mmol
n(NaOH remaining) = 55 mM × 0.1 L = 5.5 mmol
n(NaOH consumed) = 10 − 5.5 = 4.5 mmol
n(TAG) = n(NaOH) / 3 = 1.5 mmol = 0.0015 mol
M = m / n = 1.21 g / 0.0015 mol = ?
n(NaOH consumed): mmol
n(TAG): mmol
Molecular weight of TAG: g/mol
n(NaOH initial) = 0.1 L × 0.1 mol/L = 0.01 mol = 10 mmol
n(NaOH remaining) = 0.1 L × 0.055 mol/L = 5.5 mmol
n(NaOH consumed) = 10 − 5.5 = 4.5 mmol
n(TAG) = 4.5 / 3 = 1.5 mmol = 0.0015 mol
M = 1.21 g / 0.0015 mol = 807 g/mol

For comparison: triolein (C₅₇H₁₀₄O₆) M = 885 g/mol; tripalmitin (C₅₁H₉₈O₆) M = 807 g/mol ✓
